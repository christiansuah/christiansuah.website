About Us

Kpatawee, is renowned for its beautiful waterfall and rich natural environment. The area is part of the Upper Guinean Rainforest and is a branch of the St. John River, one of Liberia’s major rivers. Historically, the Kpatawee region has been significant for its natural resources and biodiversity. The local community has long utilized the area for various activities such as palm wine production, hunting, fishing, and basket making. In recent years, Kpatawee has gained attention for its eco-tourism potential The Kpatawee Waterfall Ecolodge promotes sustainable tourism and supports the local economy by employing local artisans and using locally grown food. Additionally, the Kpatawee Rice Project, a collaboration between the governments of Liberia and China, introduced new rice farming methods to discourage shifting cultivation and promote sustainable agriculture. The combination of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and sustainable practices makes Kpatawee a unique and valuable destination in Liberia.
